Oakland was once a thriving sports town, home to three major professional sports teams.

But then the Raiders left.

Followed by the Warriors.

And just last season, the A's played their final game at the Coliseum.

Leaving Oakland a teamless graveyard.

So how did the city get here and what is to come of professional sports in Oakland?

The Roots

The Oakland Coliseum, briefly dormant, came alive again when the Oakland Roots announced at the start of this season that they would claim the Oakland Coliseum as their home field. While it was planned as a short term focus for the Roots, the team has shifted their focus to the Coliseum becoming their permanent home field.

The Oakland community was left heartbroken in April 2024 when the Oakland Athletics — whose slogan read “Rooted in Oakland” — announced they would be uprooted to Sacramento for their 2025 season. They’ll play at Sutter Health Park in West Sacramento until 2027 while they wait for their new stadium in Vegas to be built.

But the Roots have revitalized the spirit of the community by bringing a refreshing and much-needed sense of energy back into the local sports scene. Their exciting, gritty style of play resonates deeply with Oakland residents, reflecting the same toughness and resilience that fans have long associated with teams that played at the Coliseum.

In 1964, Dave Newhouse, fresh out of college with a journalism degree, landed a job at the Oakland Tribune. Over a career that spanned nearly five decades before his retirement in 2011, Newhouse developed a deep understanding of the Oakland community.

“But one thing about Oakland, it's feisty,” Newhouse said. “You know, It may not be recognized, but it's feisty and it battles.”

Whether it was the hard nosed style of the Raiders, the fearless attitude of the A’s, or the passionate intensity of the Warriors, Oakland sports have always embodied a sense of pride, perseverance, and authenticity.

The Roots, a USL team founded in Oakland in 2018, continue that tradition, offering the community a team that mirrors its identity: hardworking, fearless, and proud. Their presence goes beyond just what happens on the field; they have reconnected the city with a sense of belonging and hope at a time when the loss of other major teams left a void in Oakland’s sporting landscape.

Camden Riley, defender and midfielder for the Roots, knows how important it is for him and the club to be active in the community.

“I commonly am in the community going to soccer practices, trying to help out kids and try and just grow a passion for the game,” Riley said. “Whether it be soccer or another sport in Oakland.”

Riley’s presence along with the Roots at local practices is about more than just teaching a sport; it's about nurturing dreams in a city that has always fought to keep them alive. By inspiring kids to find their passion, they are helping to build a future where sports are not just an outlet, but a lifeline.

In Oakland, where grit and heart have always been part of the fabric, leaders Riley remind individuals that true change begins in the small moments. In many ways, the Roots are not just playing soccer: they are carrying on a cultural legacy that has defined Oakland sports for generations.
